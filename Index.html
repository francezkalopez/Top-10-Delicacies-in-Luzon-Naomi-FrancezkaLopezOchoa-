Top 10 Delicacies in Luzon

Hover over each photo to learn more about these delicious dishes!

Pork Adobo

From Philippine — pork (usually pork belly or shoulder), soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and black peppercorns.

Sinigang na baboy

pork (like belly or ribs), a souring agent such as tamarind soup mix or fresh tamarind, and a variety of vegetables like tomatoes, onions, radish (labanos), and long beans (sitaw)

Kare kare

From pampanga,oxtail or beef, vegetables like eggplant, long beans, and bok choy, and the signature peanut sauce, which is made with peanut butter and ground peanuts

Laing

From Bicol — dried taro leaves cooked in coconut milk, chili, and shrimp paste.

sisig

boiled and chopped pork (like ears, face, or belly), chicken liver, onions, garlic, and chili peppers, all seasoned with soy sauce, vinegar, and calamansi or lime juice.

Pili nuts

From bicol pili nuts, sugar, and various other ingredients like syrup, butter, oil, flour, eggs, vanilla, and salt.

pinakbet

vegetables like eggplant (\(talong\)), okra, bitter melon (\(ampalaya\)), squash (\(kalabasa\)), and string beans (\(sitaw\)), seasoned with shrimp paste (\(bagoong\) or \(alamang\)).

Bulalo

Batangas’ beef marrow soup with corn and vegetables — hearty and flavorful.

Bibingka

Soft, bite-sized rice cakes from Pangasinan — chewy, sweet, and addicting!

Pancit Lucban

miki Lucban noodles, a protein like pork belly and/or shrimp, and a variety of vegetables such as chayote, carrots, and pechay.
